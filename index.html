About

Hi! My name is Rebecca. I’m a user experience professional with a background in business analysis. I have a degree in economics which gave me the foundation to study the mechanics and psychology behind consumer/user decision making.

I worked on one of the largest software digitization projects the U.S. government has undertaken. At the Veterans Affairs Financial Services Center, I transformed a convoluted web app into a streamlined, intuitive tool.

I used my active listening and problem solving skills to make usability enhancements for a small music education software startup.

I am familiar with the red tape and immense amounts of collaboration it takes to survive political project environments. I also understand the resource limitations bootstrapped startups have. I am a team player who strikes a balance between user advocacy and stakeholder needs.

I love coffee, cooking, and bike riding. My fiancé and I just returned from backpacking around Asia so I have a rested mind and a drive to dig into some fun UX challenges.

If you are looking for someone like me to join your team, please reach out below. I’d be happy to chat any time to see if I’m the right fit!

Medical Record Digitization
Software Project

Summary

I wore several hats during the nearly 3 years I worked on a software project for the VA. One of my roles was to oversee user support so I knew firsthand how frustrated the end users were with the product. It was my passion to fix that. I saw the web app pieced together over the years. It was convoluted and confusing.

I dug deeper to conduct more research, create new designs and had the opportunity to present my findings to leadership. I advocated for the user to a wide audience of directors, project managers, lead developers and business analysts.The VA is now using my designs and UX recommendations.

Note Recognition
Mobile App

Summary

A friend of mine runs a music education software service and decided to create his first mobile app as a free side feature. He had the functionality mostly figured out but after seeing a rudimentary version, I volunteered to help him with the usability and design.

Process

We sat down to discuss his implementation goals and challenges. I sketched out some options for more user friendly buttons, walked through the feedback responses resulting from user actions, as well as placement and iconography for existing navigation and branding from his full website.

I created a paper prototype and conducted some very informal user tests on several family members and friends.

VA Healthcare Benefits
Usability Assessment

Summary

During my final week at my former job I gave a presentation to several levels of leadership above my direct boss on the usability and future of our project’s web app. Afterwards, the Director of Financial Healthcare Services for the VA pulled me aside and asked if I could take a look at another project under his purview.

Process

I met with the project manager and then conducted a usability assessment of their website and application. I created a report pointing out some of the issues I found as well as some quick wins they could implement to enhance the user experience. Results
